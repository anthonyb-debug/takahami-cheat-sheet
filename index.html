Process & Work Flows
// Follow these steps for every claim review and action
1

Access the Spreadsheet or Account

You will be advised to access a spreadsheet or an account will be given to you directly by the Onshore Manager.

2

Review ALL Claims

ALL CLAIMS need to be reviewed — Status, Claim Notes, Appeal Documents, Signed Contracts, Web Portals.

3

Check Claim Statuses

If status of all claims are correct, jump to Step 5 to choose which process you're about to do. Otherwise, proceed with the next step.

4

Request Onshore Approval

Request approval from the onshore manager which claims you will be changing and for what reason. Once approved, proceed to Step 5. If not, endorse the claims to the Onshore Manager.

5

Choose Your Process

Your process will depend on whether negotiation is allowed, especially if there is a Pricing Vendor (TPA).

5.A — NEGOTIATIONS

Identify which Pricing Vendor to contact and how to submit the negotiation inquiry. See the Pricing Vendors tab for contact info.

5.B — APPEALS

View the list of carriers and workflow in creating the appeal. See the Carriers tab for contact info including Appeal TFL and TAT.

HIPAA Reference
// Covered Entities, Business Associates, and compliance overview

📋 Covered Entities

Covered entities include health plans, health care clearinghouses, and health care providers who store or transmit any health information in electronic form in connection with a HIPAA transaction (billing, insurance inquiries, etc.).

🤝 Business Associates (BAs)

Business Associates provide services to Covered Entities and may come into contact with PHI. Some BAs may use patient data for research or quality analysis. Others may encounter it as part of IT support, accreditation, practice management, quality assurance, legal services, or document shredding. Covered Entities are required to identify their Business Associates and have them sign contracts.

BA Responsibilities

Business Associates' responsibilities under HIPAA arise through statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements — usually in the form of required Business Associate Agreements (BAAs). BAs often use subcontractors (sub-BAs) who also have direct HIPAA requirements. Cloud service providers, data storage facilities, and paper storage companies all have HIPAA responsibilities even if they do not access the PHI or hold an encryption key.

⚙️ HIPAA Security Rule

Implementation specifications vary considerably in complexity. Some requirements are straightforward (e.g., designating a security compliance officer), while others are more complex (e.g., creating an Emergency Mode Operations Plan). The HITECH Act requires Business Associates and their subcontractors to comply to the same extent as Covered Entities.

Required Specifications

Must be implemented — no exceptions. These are mandatory compliance items for all Covered Entities and BAs.

Addressable Specifications

Not strictly optional. Covered Entities and BAs must either implement the specification or document why it does not apply and describe any alternate procedure being used.

🔍 Identifying Business Associates

A Business Associate is a person or organization that performs services for a Covered Entity involving the use or disclosure of PHI. Prior to HITECH (2009), CEs bore responsibility for all breaches caused by their BAs. HITECH extended federal enforcement and penalties directly to BAs. In 2016, the OCR clarified that data centers and cloud services storing ePHI are HIPAA Business Associates even if they never access the data.

BA Functions & Activities

Claims processing or administration · Data analysis, processing, or administration · Utilization review · Quality assurance · Billing · Benefit management · Practice management · Repricing · Legal, actuarial, accounting, consulting, data aggregation · IT/EHR support · Document shredding · Technology disposal · Accreditation · Financial services

CE vs. BA Distinction

If two CEs collaborate on patient treatment or payment, neither is considered a BA. Clinicians are almost always CEs, not BAs — unless acting as consultants. A BA only accesses PHI as part of services rendered to a CE. Individuals and organizations directly involved in patient treatment, payment, or operations are CEs.

📂 Examples of Business Associates

Common Examples

• Computer hardware / MSPs with PHI access
• Software vendors (e.g., EHR companies)
• Third-party claims administrators
• CPA firms with PHI access
• Attorneys providing legal services involving PHI
• Consultants performing utilization reviews

Additional Examples

• Independent medical transcriptionists
• Pharmacy benefits managers
• Accreditation agencies (e.g., JCAHO)
• Data centers / cloud services storing PHI
• Billing services with PHI access
• Non-facility consultants (HIM, Dietary, Infection Control)
• Shredding services for paper records
• Phone system support companies (storing patient voicemails/call recordings)
